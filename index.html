Korea Guide
Culture & etiquette tips
🙇
Bowing
A slight bow (15°) is a polite greeting. Deeper bows show more respect. You don't need to bow to peers your own age.
🥢
Dining etiquette
Wait for the eldest to eat first. Don't stick chopsticks upright in rice — it resembles funeral rites. Pour drinks for others, not yourself.
🚇
Subway rules
Stay quiet on the subway — phone calls are frowned upon. Priority seats (pink/blue) are strictly for elderly, pregnant, or disabled passengers.
🛍️
Shopping
Bargaining is normal at traditional markets (like Namdaemun). Fixed-price stores like Olive Young or convenience stores don't negotiate.
🚫
Things to avoid
Don't write names in red ink — it's associated with death. Avoid blowing your nose at the table. Remove shoes when entering homes.
💳
Payments
Korea is nearly cashless — cards and apps are accepted almost everywhere. Convenience stores, taxis, and markets all take card. Keep ₩10,000 cash just in case.
🗣️
